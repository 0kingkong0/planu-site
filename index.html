Coming Soon · High School

High school students don't need a planner. They need a coach.

Every competitor builds for college students. None of them serve high school. And the problem is fundamentally different — high schoolers don't have five syllabi to organize. They've never been taught how to study.

PlanU's AI Study Coach doesn't just track what's due — it teaches students how to prepare for a biology exam versus a history essay versus a math test. Assessment-specific, science-backed study strategies embedded directly into the workflow.

AI Study Coach · Preview
I have a biology midterm Friday. Chapters 4–7. I don't know where to start.
Before we touch a plan — quick check. Is the test multiple choice, short answer, or both? Different formats, completely different study moves.
Both. Mostly short answer.
Then I have to be honest with you: rereading the chapter is the worst thing you can do. Short answer tests production — pulling concepts out of your head. Rereading builds recognition, which feels like learning but isn't.
Try this instead, right now, 20 minutes: close the book. On a blank page, sketch the cellular respiration cycle from Ch 5 from memory — every arrow, every step. Wherever you stall, that's the real gap. Open the book only to fill it in. This is called retrieval practice, and it predicts test scores better than any other study method we've measured.
Do that for one chapter today. Tomorrow we layer spaced repetition on top so it sticks. Sound good?
